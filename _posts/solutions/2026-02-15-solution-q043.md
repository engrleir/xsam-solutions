## Problem Statement

Calculate the maximum moment for a simply supported beam.

## Solution

### Given
- Beam length: $L = 6 \text{ m}$
- Uniform load: $w = 10 \text{ kN/m}$

### Step 1: Calculate maximum moment

The maximum moment occurs at midspan:

$$
M_{max} = \frac{wL^2}{8}
$$

Substituting values:

$$
M_{max} = \frac{10 \times 6^2}{8} = \frac{360}{8} = 45 \text{ kN·m}
$$

### Answer

The maximum moment is $M_{max} = 45 \text{ kN·m}$ at the center of the beam.
